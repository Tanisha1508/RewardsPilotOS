# Amex Platinum Travel base earning

The India-issued American Express Platinum Travel Credit Card earns 1 Membership Rewards Point per INR 50 spent. This card is distinct from Amex Platinum Charge (1 MR per INR 40), Amex Gold (1 MR per INR 50 including fuel and utilities), Amex Platinum Reserve (1 MR per INR 50 excluding fuel, insurance, utilities), the Amex Membership Rewards Credit Card, and Amex SmartEarn (1 MR per INR 50 base with 5X Amazon and 10X select merchants) — data must never be merged across these products. Source: americanexpress.com/in official pages, retrieved 2026-07-20.

# Amex Platinum Travel earning exclusions

No points are earned on fuel, insurance, utilities (electricity, water, gas), cash transactions, and EMI conversion at point of sale. The EMI exclusion applies specifically to merchant-website or merchant-terminal EMI selection, effective 2020-03-15; EMI via Amex SafeKey and post-purchase EMI conversion still earn normally. Source: official pages, retrieved 2026-07-20.

# Amex Platinum Travel welcome gift

10,000 Membership Rewards points, first cardmembership year only, requiring BOTH INR 15,000 spend within 90 days AND payment of the annual fee. Source: official pages, retrieved 2026-07-20.

# Amex Platinum Travel milestones

Three recurring milestone tiers per cardmembership year, redeemable specifically on the Platinum Travel Collection: 7,500 bonus MR points at INR 1,90,000 spend; 10,000 bonus MR points plus a Taj Experiences E-Gift Card worth INR 10,000 at INR 4,00,000; and an additional 22,500 bonus MR points at INR 7,00,000. Operational note: milestone points split into threshold and bonus portions — the bonus portion is auto-issued only for milestones achieved after 2026-03-09; earlier achievements require a manual claim via call or chat. Source: official pages, retrieved 2026-07-20.

# Amex Reward Multiplier accelerated earning

Purchases through the Reward Multiplier portal at eligible merchants (Flipkart, Tata Cliq, Ajio, Nykaa, Myntra, Croma, Tanishq, Samsung, Giva, Candere, Apple, MakeMyTrip, Etihad Airways, plus a separate e-voucher platform) earn 3X total points (1X base plus 2X bonus) per INR 50; direct merchant-site purchases do not qualify. The bonus portion is capped at 25,000 bonus MR points per month; base 1X earning is uncapped and separate. Points credit within 120 business days (hotel and airline merchants: within 120 days of service date). The program's defined validity is 2021-01-01 to 2026-07-31; renewal status is unconfirmed as the end date approaches. Broad per-merchant exclusions apply (mobile phones, jewellery and precious metals, large appliances, gift cards, flight/utility/insurance payments, gaming consoles, varying by merchant); per-merchant exclusion logic is a documented MVP limitation. Source: official Reward Multiplier T&C, retrieved 2026-07-20.

# Amex Platinum Travel fees

The annual fee is INR 5,000 plus GST, the same in the first year and from the second year onward (no separate joining/renewal split; official, retrieved 2026-07-20). The foreign currency markup is 3.5 percent plus GST, approximately 4.4 to 4.5 percent effective, uniform across Amex India cards (three independent trackers plus a cardholder-reported real transaction rate, converging).

No official spend-based renewal fee waiver exists (three independent sources — CardInsider, Paisabazaar, CardMaven — explicitly stating this in near-identical language, retrieved 2026-07-20). Informal customer-retention offers exist case-by-case: calling Amex near renewal can yield a discretionary partial waiver or a points-for-fee trade (for example, surrendering 10,000 MR points for a full waiver), but this is retention negotiation, not published policy. A spend-based waiver existed historically around 2018 with an INR 15 lakh threshold and was discontinued; it does not apply to current rules.

# Amex Membership Rewards points expiry

Membership Rewards points never expire while the card is active and in good standing, effective 2022-05-03. Upon cancellation, points are forfeited within 30 days. Source: official, retrieved 2026-07-20.

# Amex Membership Rewards point value by redemption path

Statement credit via "Select & Redeem — Cover Your Card Charges": INR 0.25 per point, the general Amex India rate (two independent India-specific sources agreeing). The card-specific Platinum Travel Collection catalogue offers preferential tiered rates: Air India vouchers at INR 0.30 per point (20,000 points = INR 6,000; 40,000 points = INR 12,000), Taj Hotels and Resorts at INR 0.40 per point (12,500 points = INR 5,000; 25,000 points = INR 10,000), and The Postcard Hotels at INR 0.50 per point (20,000 points = INR 10,000) — exact figures from the official catalogue page, retrieved 2026-07-20. Transfer redemption value is not a fixed figure and varies by partner.

# Amex Membership Rewards transfer partners (India)

Airline partners, all at 2:1 (2 MR = 1 partner mile): Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer (minimum 800 MR), Emirates Skywards (800), Cathay Pacific Asia Miles (800), British Airways Avios (1,200), Qatar Airways Avios (500), and Virgin Atlantic (800). Hotel partners: Marriott Bonvoy at 1:1 (minimum 100 MR) and Hilton Honors at 1:0.90 (minimum 1,000 MR). A maximum of 9,00,000 MR points can be transferred per redemption transaction or per calendar year. Source: India-specific convergence across CardTrail, Rivo, and CardInsider; the official India transfer portal is login-gated, and Amex's UK/EU International Currency Card pages were explicitly excluded as a different product and market.

# Amex Platinum Travel and Air India

There is NO direct transfer partner for Air India: Club Vistara was removed following the Vistara/Air India merger and no replacement was added. Membership Rewards points cannot be transferred to Air India Flying Returns, directly or via a workaround.
